Hi! I'm Alicia Sedlock and I'm a front-end architect.

I love to build things for people. I focus a lot of energy into making sure what I build is accessible to everyone. I love web standards and CSS, and get excited about front-end architecture, CSS selector naming strategies, and performance. I can geek out about nitty gritty details about W3C specifications, as well as discuss the hollistics of a project's architecture. Boston is a massive hub in the web industry, which makes it a perfect home for me.

I enjoy highly collaborative environments where I can get feedback and advice from peers, as well as lead technical discussions and implementations. Whether I'm pair programming, doing code reviews, or just spitballing potential solutions for a problem, I don't like to work in a silo. I care greatly for user experience and like to have insight or involvement in wireframing and other UX tasks.
